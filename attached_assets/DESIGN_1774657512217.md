# Design System Strategy: Aviation Intelligence Portal

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
**Creative North Star: The Precision Navigator**

In the world of aviation, precision is not a luxury—it is the baseline. This design system departs from generic "SaaS dashboards" by adopting an editorial, high-fidelity aesthetic that mirrors the sophisticated instrumentation of a modern flight deck. We move beyond simple blue boxes to create an environment defined by **Tonal Depth** and **Asymmetric Authority**.

The "Precision Navigator" aesthetic is achieved by prioritizing white space as a functional element, utilizing a hierarchy of surfaces rather than lines, and employing high-contrast typography that feels curated. This is not just a data repository; it is a command center designed for rapid cognitive processing and high-end visual appeal.

---

## 2. Colors
Our palette is anchored in deep oceanic blues and crisp aeronautical whites, accented by vibrant, high-saturation indicators for critical system statuses.

### The "No-Line" Rule
To maintain a premium, modern feel, **1px solid borders are prohibited for sectioning.** Boundaries must be defined exclusively through background color shifts. For example, a `surface-container-low` data table should sit directly on a `background` page without a stroke. Contrast is the new border.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Treat the UI as a physical stack of semi-transparent layers. 
- **Base Layer:** `surface` (#f7f9fb)
- **Primary Content Areas:** `surface-container-low` (#f2f4f6)
- **Interactive Cards:** `surface-container-lowest` (#ffffff) for maximum "lift."
- **Overlays/Modals:** Use `surface-bright` with a 15% opacity drop for a glass effect.

### The "Glass & Gradient" Rule
Floating elements (e.g., flight status badges, quick-action menus) should utilize Glassmorphism. Apply `surface-container-lowest` at 80% opacity with a `backdrop-blur: 12px`. For main CTAs and "Hero" cards (e.g., Total Airlines), use linear gradients transitioning from `on-primary-container` (#4f83fe) to `primary` (#000a28) at a 135-degree angle.

---

## 3. Typography
We use a dual-font strategy to balance technical authority with modern readability.

*   **Display & Headlines (Manrope):** Chosen for its wide stance and geometric precision. These should be tracked slightly tighter (-2%) to feel like a high-end magazine header.
*   **Body & Labels (Inter):** The industry standard for high-density data. It provides exceptional legibility at small scales (e.g., `label-sm`).

**Hierarchy Strategy:**
- **Display-LG (3.5rem):** Reserved for "Aha!" moments—total fleet counts or global registry metrics.
- **Headline-SM (1.5rem):** Used for primary section titles.
- **Title-SM (1rem):** Bold weight used for card headers.
- **Body-MD (0.875rem):** The workhorse for all registry data and descriptive text.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth
Depth in this system is a product of light and material, not artificial outlines.

*   **The Layering Principle:** Avoid shadows for static elements. Instead, place a `surface-container-lowest` card on a `surface-container` background to create a soft, natural lift.
*   **Ambient Shadows:** For interactive "hover" states or floating action buttons, use an extra-diffused shadow: `box-shadow: 0 12px 32px -4px rgba(25, 28, 30, 0.06)`. Note the tint—the shadow uses the `on-surface` color, not pure black.
*   **Ghost Borders:** If data density requires a container boundary, use the `outline-variant` (#c5c6cf) at **15% opacity**. It should be felt, not seen.

---

## 5. Components

### Cards & Data Lists
*   **Style:** `DEFAULT` (8px) or `md` (12px) rounded corners.
*   **Constraint:** Forbid divider lines between list items. Use vertical spacing (Scale `4` or `5`) or alternating row tints using `surface-container-low` and `surface-container-lowest`.
*   **Visual Soul:** Use a vertical accent bar (4px width) on the left of a card using `primary` or a status color (e.g., `error`) to denote urgency without cluttering the card with text labels.

### Buttons & Chips
*   **Primary Button:** Gradient-filled (Primary to Primary-Container) with `lg` (16px) rounding.
*   **Status Chips:** Use high-chroma backgrounds with high-contrast text (e.g., `on-tertiary-container` on `tertiary-fixed`). The contrast ratio must exceed 7:1 for instant recognition.

### Input Fields
*   **Style:** Soft, pill-shaped (`full` roundedness) or `md` (12px).
*   **State:** Use `surface-container-highest` for the background and `surface-tint` for the active focus ring (no solid border).

### Registry Indicators (Custom)
*   **Aviation Density Badge:** Small, high-contrast badges for IATA/ICAO codes. Use `secondary-container` backgrounds to ensure they don't compete with primary actions.

---

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do
*   **DO** use asymmetric layouts. If a dashboard has three columns, make one 50% and the others 25% to create an editorial flow.
*   **DO** use "surface-tint" (#0c56d0) for interactive icons to signal playfulness within a professional framework.
*   **DO** prioritize high-density data through the use of `label-sm` for secondary metadata (e.g., "Last Updated" timestamps).

### Don't
*   **DON'T** use 100% black text. Always use `on-surface` (#191c1e) to keep the UI feeling "expensive" and soft.
*   **DON'T** use standard 1px grey dividers. They break the "Precision Navigator" immersion. Use white space (`Spacing 6`) instead.
*   **DON'T** use sharp 0px corners. Every element must feel machined and polished, requiring at least the `DEFAULT` (8px) radius.
